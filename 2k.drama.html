About Korean Dramas


When you feel like your life isn't dramatic enough, or dramatic in the way that you want, why not distract yourself with a melodramatic and highly engaging… drama?

5 Korean Dramas To Try

Autumn In My Heart (2000)

One of the first Korean Dramas to set off the K Drama craze, Autumn In My Heart tells a tragic tale about two young girls switched at birth. This intricate drama spans over a decade, and starts with both families finding out their daughters (Eun Suh and Shin Ae) had been switched by a twist of fate, in the hospital they were born in. Growing up on opposite sides of the tracks, they lived very different lives, even while attending the same high school. When kind hearted Eun Suh finds out, she insists that she and Shin Ae are switched back. This drama brings about a maelstrom of emotions, stemming from illness, love triangles, and forbidden love.

Winter Sonata (2002)

Following the trend of heady complicated dramas, Winter Sonata captured many hearts with its romance between distant Joon Sang, and childlike Yoo Jin. Accident, amnesia, and heartbreak make this drama a gripping story to follow. Winter Sonata also launched the main actor, Bae Yong Joon’s, career, and lead to obsessed fans all around Asia, bestowing him with the beloved title, "sYon-sama”.

Boys Over Flowers (2009)

Based on Japanese manga, Hana Yori Dango, this Korean live-action adaptation re-tells the classic story of poor girl meets rich boy, causing sparks. The main character, Jan Di, while attending a privileged high school on a scholarship, becomes caught in the crosshairs of the popular boys group. Especially drawing the attention of the leader, Jun Pyo, her defiance against the status quo keeps her butting heads with the vain elite of the school. Her earnest ways however, start to affect everyone around her.

Secret Garden (2010)

An entertaining drama with a magical twist. When arrogant businessman, Joo Won, accidentally meets bold stuntwoman, Ra Im, sparks fly. Their barely tolerated exchanges are full of friction, and discord, when a strange turn of events makes them switch bodies. While they struggle with hiding these bizarre events, their bond slowly grows.

Coffee Prince (2007)

Han Kyul, the owner of "Coffee Prince", a small coffee shop, decides to ramp up business by overhauling their image. Turning a dingy old coffee shop into a quaint and lovely coffee shop with handsome males as the only staff members. He unknowingly hires Eun Chan, who appears to be a young guy, but is actually just a tomboyish girl. She hides her gender to keep her job, but things get more tangled as Han Kyul develops confusing feelings for her, someone he thinks is male, and questions his sexuality.
